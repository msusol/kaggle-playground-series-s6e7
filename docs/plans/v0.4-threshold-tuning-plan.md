# v0.4 - Threshold Tuning Plan (Rung 3)

Goal: check whether per-class decision-rule tuning improves on the current best model
(v0.3 Variant 1, CatBoost on base features, OOF 0.9493, LB 0.94885) beyond plain
argmax over class probabilities. Balanced accuracy = mean of per-class recall; argmax
over raw softmax probabilities is not guaranteed to maximize that specific objective
under class imbalance, even when the model was trained with `auto_class_weights='Balanced'`.

Per `implementation-plan.md`, there may be less headroom here than originally expected
(CatBoost's per-class recalls are already fairly balanced: at-risk 0.933, fit 0.950,
unhealthy 0.965), but it's a cheap, principled thing to check before moving to Rung 4.

## Approach

Weighted argmax: `predict = argmax(proba * w)` for a 3-vector `w`, with `w[at-risk]`
fixed at 1.0 (only the ratios matter for argmax) and `w[fit]` / `w[unhealthy]` searched
to directly maximize OOF balanced accuracy.

## Steps

1. Re-run v0.3 Variant 1's exact config (same features, same 5-fold split
   `random_state=42`, same CatBoost hyperparameters/custom eval metric) — but this
   time capture per-fold `predict_proba` on the validation fold, not just hard
   `predict()` labels, to get a genuine OOF probability matrix. Sanity-check
   reproducibility against v0.3's known results (`best_iterations: [428, 950, 605,
   339, 779]`, OOF 0.9493) before trusting the new OOF probabilities.
2. Grid search (log-scale) over `w[fit]` / `w[unhealthy]` to maximize
   `balanced_accuracy_score` on the OOF weighted-argmax predictions.
3. **Nested validation** of the tuned weights: fit weights on 4/5 folds' OOF
   predictions, evaluate on the held-out 5th fold, cycle across all 5 folds. This
   guards against overfitting the 2 free parameters directly on the same OOF set used
   to claim victory — with only 2 parameters and 690k rows the risk is low, but it's
   cheap to check properly rather than assume.
4. Fit final production weights on the full OOF set (most stable estimate).
5. Apply to the already-computed test-set probabilities; compare against the current
   best (0.9493 OOF / 0.94885 LB — or 0.9491 / 0.94913 for Variant 2, they're tied).
6. Write a candidate `submission.csv` only if the tuned-threshold model is actually
   better under the nested-validation estimate, not just the same-data OOF estimate.
   Do not auto-submit to Kaggle.

## Acceptance

- OOF probability reproduction matches v0.3's known result before trusting anything
  downstream.
- The reported improvement (if any) comes from the nested-validation estimate, not
  the same-data OOF fit, to avoid a false positive from tuning 2 parameters on the
  evaluation set.
- Findings recorded in `docs/plans/leaderboard.md`, `docs/investigate/notebook-runs.md`,
  and `docs/plans/implementation-plan.md` (Rung 3 status) regardless of outcome —
  a negative result here (threshold tuning doesn't help) is as valuable to know as
  a positive one, per the v0.2 precedent.
